About

Bridging ocean science and machine learning

I'm a PhD student in the Department of Earth and Environmental Engineering at Columbia University, working in the Gentine Lab. My research focuses on understanding and improving how we model the exchanges of heat and momentum between the ocean and atmosphere — processes that are critical for weather forecasting and climate projection.

Before starting my PhD, I worked for two years as an engineering consultant in process, energy, and environmental engineering. I hold a B.S. in Chemical Engineering from Washington University in St. Louis.

My current work is supported by the NSF-funded Learning the Earth with Artificial Intelligence and Physics (LEAP) Science and Technology Center, where I combine observational data, remote sensing, and physics-informed machine learning to build better parameterizations of air-sea fluxes.

Research

Current focus areas

Exploring the physical exchanges at the ocean-atmosphere boundary and their implications for climate and renewable energy.

Air-Sea Flux Parameterization

Developing improved parameterizations of heat, moisture, and momentum fluxes at the ocean-atmosphere interface. By combining in situ observations, satellite remote sensing, and physics-informed machine learning, I work to reduce biases in bulk flux algorithms used by weather and climate models.

Heat Flux Momentum Transfer Bulk Algorithms ML Parameterization

Offshore Wind Energy

Investigating how air-sea interactions influence the marine atmospheric boundary layer in regions planned for offshore wind development. Understanding these dynamics is essential for accurate wind resource assessment, turbine performance prediction, and the long-term viability of offshore wind farms.

Methods

Research approach

Integrating observational data with computational techniques to advance understanding of ocean-atmosphere coupling.

In Situ & Remote Sensing

Leveraging ship-based measurements, buoy networks, and satellite observations to build comprehensive datasets of air-sea exchange.

Machine Learning

Applying physics-informed neural networks and other ML techniques to discover patterns and improve parameterizations of complex flux processes.

Climate Modeling

Integrating improved parameterizations into Earth system models to enhance the fidelity of weather and climate simulations.
